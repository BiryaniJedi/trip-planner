## Notes

- **Best time to book flights:** 3-6 months before departure; for April 2026, start booking by October–November 2025 for best prices and seat availability.  
- **Weather:** Early to mid-April in Tokyo is mild (60–70°F/16–22°C), perfect for outdoor activities, hiking, and temple visits. Cherry blossoms (sakura) typically bloom in late March-early April but can last through mid-April depending on the year.  
- **Currency:** Japanese Yen (JPY). Current conversion approx. 1 USD = 130 JPY (subject to fluctuation).  
- **Electricity:** 100V Type A plugs (same as US, no adapter needed).  
- **Language:** Japanese primarily; English is widely spoken in tourist areas but learning basic Japanese phrases is helpful.  
- **Connectivity:** Rent a pocket WiFi at the airport or use international roaming.

## Itinerary

### Day 1: Arrival and Settle In
- **Morning:** Arrive at Narita/Haneda Airport, get a Suica or Pasmo card.  
- **Afternoon:** Check-in hotel in Shinjuku; rest and freshen up.  
- **Evening:** Explore Shinjuku’s vibrant nightlife, visit Omoide Yokocho for casual eats.  
- **Dinner:** Yakitori at Torikizoku (affordable chicken skewers chain).

---

### Day 2: Explore Asakusa and Senso-ji Temple

- **14:00:** River cruise on Sumida River to see cherry blossoms.  
- **16:00:** Visit Tokyo Skytree nearby (observatory).  
- **19:00:** Dinner at Daikokuya Tempura (classic tempura restaurant).

## Restaurant Recommendations (Highlights)

- **Torikizoku:** Casual yakitori chain, good value and tasty skewers.  
- **Asakusa Menchi:** Iconic deep-fried minced meat croquettes, must-try street snack.  
- **Daikokuya Tempura:** Traditional tempura restaurant famous for its crispy batter.  
- **Ichiran Ramen:** Customize your ramen bowl, solitary dining experience.  
- **Tsukiji Outer Market:** Fresh sushi, sashimi and Japanese street food stalls.  
- **Afuri Ramen:** Known for light yuzu-based ramen broth.  
- **Uobei Sushi:** Cost-effective conveyor belt sushi with interactive ordering.  
- **Tonkatsu Maisen:** Premium tonkatsu in elegant setting near Shibuya.

## Travel Tips

- Purchase a Pasmo or Suica card on arrival for easy transit via trains and buses.  
- Reserve tickets for popular attractions (teamLab, Ghibli Museum) well in advance.  
- Carry cash as many small vendors do not accept credit cards, but most restaurants and shops do.  
- Use Google Maps with transit for easy navigation.  
- Practice basic Japanese greetings and thank you (Konnichiwa, Arigatou gozaimasu).  
- Beware of busy hours in trains (morning 7-9 am and evening 5-7 pm).  
- Connect with free Wi-Fi or rent a pocket Wi-Fi device at the airport for convenience.
